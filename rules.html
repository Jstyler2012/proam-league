A. PGA Tour Pro Selection

  • Each week, every player drafts one PGA Tour professional as their “Playing Partner.”
  • Your pro’s score is their total score to par at the conclusion of that week’s PGA Tour event.
  • If your pro misses the cut, your pro’s score defaults to the cut line score, not their actual score.
Example (missed cut):
If you select a player who finishes +1 but the cut line is -1, your pro score for the week is -1.

B. Your Personal Score

  • You may submit multiple 18-hole rounds during the week (Monday–Sunday).
  • Your lowest (best) round to par for that week is the score that counts.
  • Only 18-hole rounds qualify.

C. Weekly Score Calculation

Weekly Total = Best 18-Hole Score to Par + Pro’s Final Score to Par
Lowest combined score wins.
Full scoring example:
Your best 18-hole round this week: +4
Your drafted pro finishes: -6
Weekly Total = (+4) + (-6) = -2

A. Draft Timing

  • The weekly draft opens Monday at 12:00 PM (or immediately after the prior PGA Tour event concludes if it ends Monday).
  • You may post personal scores before drafting, but your pro must be selected before the weekly draft deadline.

B. Draft Order

  • Draft order is determined by highest handicap first, lowest handicap last (among active participants that week).

C. Changing Picks

  • Pro selections may be swapped only after all participants have drafted.
  • All changes must be finalized by 11:59 PM the day before the PGA Tour event begins.

D. Withdrawals

  • If your pro withdraws after the draft deadline or during the tournament, your pro score defaults to the cut line score.

Points are awarded weekly and accumulate toward season standings.

A. Standard (Non-Major) Weeks

  • 1st: 10 points
  • 2nd: 8 points
  • 3rd: 5 points
  • 4th and below: 1 participation point

B. Major Weeks (2x Points)

  • 1st: 20 points
  • 2nd: 16 points
  • 3rd: 10 points
  • 4th: 5 points
  • 5th: 3 points
  • 6th and below: 2 participation points

C. Championship Week (3x Points)

  • 1st: 30 points
  • 2nd: 24 points
  • 3rd: 15 points
  • 4th: 8 points
  • 5th: 5 points
  • 6th and below: 2 participation points

Applies to both weekly contests and season standings.

  1. Higher handicap wins
  2. If still tied:
    • Weekly: lowest personal 18-hole score (excluding pro score)
    • Season: lowest personal round recorded during the season
Example:
Two players tie on Weekly Total at -3. Player A is a 12 handicap, Player B is an 8 handicap.
Winner = Player A (higher handicap).

A. Verified Handicap

  • All players must use a USGA GHIN handicap at the start of the season.
  • Handicap remains fixed for the full season.

B. No GHIN Handicap

  • Best score over last 3 rounds will establish a starting handicap.
  • Handicap may be adjusted every 2 weeks based on submitted scores.

C. Transparency

  • All handicaps will be displayed beside each player’s name throughout the season.

A. Standard Weeks

  • $15 buy-in
  • $10 → Weekly pot
  • $5 → Season pot
  • Payment due before: posting first round OR 11:59 PM before tournament begins (whichever comes first)
Weekly payout: 1st 50% · 2nd 30% · 3rd 20%

B. Major Weeks

  • $25 buy-in
  • $20 → Weekly pot
  • $5 → Season pot
Payout: 1st 50% · 2nd 30% · 3rd 20%

C. Championship Week

  • $50 buy-in
  • $30 → Weekly pot
  • $20 → Season pot
  • Payment due before you draft for Championship Week.
Payout: 1st 50% · 2nd 30% · 3rd 20%

(Subject to change depending on final pot total)

  • 1st: 56%
  • 2nd: 28%
  • 3rd: 16%
  • Can I submit multiple rounds? Yes — you can submit multiple 18-hole rounds (Mon–Sun). Your best round counts.
  • Do 9-hole rounds count? No — only 18-hole rounds qualify.
  • What if my pro misses the cut? Your pro score becomes the cut line score for that week.
  • What if my pro withdraws? If after the draft deadline or during the tournament, pro score defaults to the cut line score.
  • When do I need to pay? Before posting your first round OR by 11:59 PM before the tournament begins (whichever comes first). Championship week requires payment before drafting.
